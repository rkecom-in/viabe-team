# CL-389 — CORRECTION (framing): the privacy notice is a SYSTEM-LEVEL / product launch-gate

Fazal challenged a framing error and is correct. Prior framing (DSR/owner_inputs brief, CL-373 closeout tracker) treated 'the privacy notice' as a sub-deliverable OF the owner_inputs feature, gated behind owner_inputs merge gates. That scoping is BACKWARDS.

CORRECTION: The privacy obligation does not attach to the owner_inputs feature. It attaches to the CUSTOMER'S MESSAGE — once customer personal data enters Viabe, DPDP applies to every component that touches it: Orchestrator, Sales Recovery Agent, Composer, pipeline tables (retention), DBOS durability layer (~2.5h transient body hold), Anthropic LLM calls (transmission), Voyage (transmission if used), and owner_inputs. The privacy notice is therefore a SYSTEM-LEVEL / PRODUCT-LEVEL deliverable covering Viabe's whole handling of customer data — NOT an owner_inputs sub-task.

owner_inputs got attached to the privacy work only because it was the feature in flight when privacy surfaced — an accident of sequencing, not a real boundary. The OWNER_INPUTS_EXTRACTION_ENABLED switch changes ONE LINE of the notice (whether message content is also sent to Anthropic Haiku for intent classification). It does NOT scope the notice. The notice must cover the Orchestrator + Sales Recovery Agent + Composer + pipeline retention + DBOS hold + Anthropic transmission REGARDLESS of the switch state, because all of those handle customer data and are live today.

CONSEQUENCE: The privacy notice must be re-rostered as a launch-gate deliverable in its own right (a Viabe-wide requirement before production handling of real customer data), NOT a child of the owner_inputs build. It feeds owner_inputs' merge gate (iv) but is not owned by it. The earlier 'drafted after owner_inputs / gated behind owner_inputs' sequencing in CL-373 is superseded.

SECONDARY CORRECTION (same root): an earlier Clau explanation in this session described owner_inputs as 'a separate system' from the agent in a way that implied the privacy TREATMENT was also separable. That implication was wrong and Fazal caught it. The feature is separable; the privacy coverage is not — privacy coverage follows the data, and the data flows through the whole system.
