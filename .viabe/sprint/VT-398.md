## Contract (Fazal 2026-06-23 — found during Sundaram e2e). COUNSEL-GATED, do not just build.

Fazal: signup should also capture **WhatsApp consent**, and "merge all possible consents and policy
agreements into a single checkbox."

**Cowork flag (raised once; Fazal decides):** a single mandatory catch-all checkbox is a DPDP risk —
the Act requires consent that is **free, specific, informed, unbundled**. Bundling distinct *consents*
(data-processing, WhatsApp/marketing opt-in) into one tick can invalidate the consent and create
liability. Defensible pattern:
- **One line to ACCEPT policies** (Terms of Service + Privacy Policy) — policies may bundle.
- **Separate affirmative tick(s) for specific CONSENTS** — DPDP processing consent, and a distinct
  **WhatsApp messaging opt-in** (the new ask). Residency stays as configured.

## Scope (after counsel ruling on the structure):
1. Add a **WhatsApp opt-in** consent at signup (owner consents to be contacted on WhatsApp), recorded
   as its own `consent_record` with a versioned consent_text (like the DPDP/residency records).
2. Restructure the signup consent block per the counsel-approved model (policies-accept vs specific
   consents). Keep each consent's version pinned (no free strings).
3. Surface the new WhatsApp-opt-in version in the consent registry; ensure DSR + opt-out paths cover it.

## Gate
This is **C1–C3 counsel-package territory** (consent model + copy). Do NOT ship a single-checkbox
catch-all without counsel sign-off on the structure. Build only the counsel-approved version.
