# CL-119 — PR-fix-7 single-role design is structurally wrong

PR-fix-7 brief (CL-116/CL-117) assumed all 10 DB writers were tenant-scoped and could uniformly migrate behind a single app_role with FORCE RLS. Claude Code's implementation-pass surfaced this is wrong:

THREE CLASSES OF DB ACCESS, not one:
1. TENANT-SCOPED WRITERS (the original 10ish): must set app.current_tenant_id, must run under app_role with FORCE RLS. These are what the wrapper is for.
2. PRE-TENANT / CROSS-TENANT OPS: _lookup_tenant (runs before tenant_id is known — it's how we learn the tenant), _within_rate_limits (deliberately cross-tenant). These CANNOT run under FORCE RLS because there's no tenant to scope to, or the operation is intentionally global. Per migration 000b, these are the intended service-role path.
3. DBOS INTERNALS: framework-level bookkeeping on the dbos schema. Not application code, shouldn't be subject to tenant RLS.

The CL-116 single-role design forces (2) and (3) through a role that mathematically cannot service them. Brief is structurally wrong, not just incomplete.
