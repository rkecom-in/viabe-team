## What CoderC must produce
The day-39 refund-conversation flow. When the day-39 evaluator (VT-10.4) determines a tenant's ARRR < 2x cumulative fees, this flow proactively reaches out, explains the situation honestly, offers the refund, and processes the owner's decision. Fazal personally approves all copy.
1. **Trigger flow**:
	- Day-39 scheduled trigger (VT-3.5) detects eligible tenant; emits `day39_refund_eligible` event.
	- This handler at `apps/team/src/owner_surface/refund_conversation.py` consumes the event.
	- Constructs the refund offer message via `refund_offer` Meta template with structured params: tenant business name, ARRR achieved, fees paid, refund amount, decision deadline (48h).
	- Sends via VT-5.7. The 24h-window question doesn't apply because templates ship outside window by design.
2. **Honest framing in copy** (Fazal personally reviews):
	- The message states facts: "You've been on Viabe Team for 39 days. In that time, our campaigns recovered ₹X for your business. You've paid ₹Y in subscription fees. Per our day-39 commitment, since recovered revenue is less than 2x fees, you're eligible for a full refund."
	- Three options:
		1. "Reply REFUND to receive your full refund (u20b9Y) and end your subscription. The refund processes within 5 business days."
		2. "Reply CONTINUE to stay on for another billing period. We won't make another refund offer until 90 days from now."
		3. "Reply DISCUSS and Fazal will personally call you to talk about your situation."
	- No persuasion language. No "are you sure?" guilt-tripping. CodeX rejects PRs that add retention manipulation.
3. **Owner reply handling**:
	- Webhook (VT-3.3) classifies via VT-5.11. Adds intent `refund_decision` (the 13th classifier intent — PR follow-up to bump prompt to v5).
	- Reply parsed for one of three decisions:
		- `refund`: trigger VT-10.5 refund execution; transition tenant phase to `refunded`.
		- `continue`: tenant phase stays `paid_active`; record decision; suppress next refund offer for 90 days.
		- `discuss`: send Fazal a Telegram alert with tenant context; tenant gets ack "Fazal will reach out within 24 hours."
		- Unrecognized: clarifying nudge once, then escalate to Fazal if still unclear.
4. **48-hour decision window**:
	- Default 48h from message send. After timeout: send a single nudge ("Reminder: you have until \[date\] to choose REFUND, CONTINUE, or DISCUSS"). 24 hours after the nudge, if still no reply, default to CONTINUE with an audit note ("timeout default").
	- The default is `continue`, not `refund`, because automatic refund without owner consent could be financially destabilizing for the tenant; better to let them stay and offer again.
5. **Audit trail**:
	- Every refund-eligible event → `privacy_audit_log` row + `pipeline_log` event.
	- Every owner decision → audited.
	- Every refund executed → audited (in VT-10.5).
	- `docs/runbooks/day39-refund-conversations.md` documents the flow + escalation path.
6. **Tests** at `apps/team/tests/owner_surface/test_refund_conversation.py`:
	- Synthetic eligible tenant → message dispatched → owner replies REFUND → VT-10.5 invoked → phase=refunded.
	- Continue path: → phase stays paid_active; 90-day suppression flag set.
	- Discuss path: Telegram alert + owner ack.
	- Unrecognized reply: clarifying nudge; second unrecognized → Fazal escalation.
	- Timeout: 48h elapsed, nudge sent; 72h elapsed, defaulted to continue with audit note.
	- 90-day suppression: previously CONTINUE'd tenant; if eligible again before 90d, no offer sent.
	- Hindi variants for all 3 decisions tested.
	- Cross-tenant: tenant_A's refund decision can't affect tenant_B.
## Architectural rules to enforce in this subtask
- Pillar 7: refund offer is structural at day 39 (no Fazal-discretion in WHEN; only in copy review). CodeX rejects PRs that bury, soft-pedal, or condition the offer.
- Pillar 8: one refund flow. CodeX rejects parallel refund-handling code.
- 48h decision window with timeout-defaults-to-continue is intentional Phase 1 design. Type 2 to change.
- 90-day suppression after CONTINUE prevents nag spam. Type 2 to change.
- Fazal personally reviews every word of refund-offer copy + clarifying nudge + escalation messages. Documented in PR review checklist.
