## What CoderC must produce
A structured ADR set: at least 7 architectural decision records covering Phase 1's significant architectural choices. Each ADR follows MADR-lite format (Markdown Any Decision Record). Lives at `docs/adr/`. Indexed at `docs/adr/README.md`.
1. **ADR format** (template at `docs/adr/0000-template.md`):
	- Title (4-6 words capturing the decision)
	- Status: proposed / accepted / deprecated / superseded by \[link\]
	- Context (2-4 paragraphs on why this decision was needed)
	- Considered Options (3-5 options with pros/cons)
	- Decision (the chosen option + rationale)
	- Consequences (positive + negative + neutral)
	- References (Pillar(s), related VT subtasks, external sources)
	- Date + author
2. **Required ADRs (≥7)**:
	- **ADR-0001: LangGraph as orchestrator framework** — vs CrewAI (Reports'), vs LangChain agents, vs custom. Why LangGraph: state-machine semantics, explicit transitions, resumability via Postgres checkpointer.
	- **ADR-0002: Three-layer tenant isolation** — RLS + typed wrappers + agent context, each independently sufficient. Why three: defense in depth; one failure doesn't compromise tenant boundary.
	- **ADR-0003: K-anonymity ≥10 at L3 construction (build-time invariant)** — Phase 1 ships at k=10 per concept doc Section 10 locked Type 3 commitment ("well above typical regulatory k=3-5 — we have headroom"). The May 4 transient decision to ship at k=5 is SUPERSEDED. Rationale (concept doc): k=10 produces a stronger trust signal in customer-facing marketing, gives DPDP Act May 2027 headroom, and the 180-day quarantine + locality coarsening + business_type axis combination already addresses the small-cohort emission concern (most cohorts won't fire L3 in Phase 1 regardless of whether k=5 or k=10 — with 10 design partners, no cohort hits k=10 either, so empty-pattern fallback path is what governs Phase 1 behavior either way). Lowering k below 10 is forbidden Type 3 commitment. Raising k above 10 is always allowed.
	- **ADR-0004: 180-day quarantine for new tenants in cross-tenant aggregations** — prevents new tenants from being identifiable contributors before sufficient cohort dilution.
	- **ADR-0005: Card capture at conversion (not sign-up)** — honest trial signal vs friction reduction vs fraud risk; Phase 1 chooses honest trial.
	- **ADR-0006: Day-39 deterministic evaluator** — SQL-only, byte-identical reproducibility; no LLM judgment in refund eligibility.
	- **ADR-0007: One Director per pipeline run (Reports precedent)** — NOT applicable to Team Phase 1 directly because Team's agent is the Sales Recovery Agent which has its own context bundle; ADR records why Team doesn't yet have a Director equivalent + when to revisit (Phase 2 multi-specialist orchestration).
	- **ADR-0008: Cookie-free A/B framework via IP+UA hash** — vs cookie-based; privacy posture trade-off.
	- **ADR-0009: 22 Meta WhatsApp templates as launch dependency** — captures the launch-bottleneck reality + the 6-8 week lead time as a structural fact for future launches.
	- **ADR-0010: No KYC at sign-up Phase 1** — captures VT-13.7 decision rationale + re-evaluation triggers.
	- At least 7 of the above 10 must be authored Phase 1; remaining can be Phase 2 deferments.
3. **Index** at `docs/adr/README.md`:
	- List of all ADRs with status + date.
	- Categorical view: by Pillar, by subsystem, by sprint.
	- Maintenance: every architectural decision → new ADR before implementation lands.

## Architectural rules to enforce in this subtask
- Pillar 8: ONE ADR repository. CodeX rejects scattered architectural rationale documents.

- ADRs not deleted; superseded.
- Phase 1 minimum 7 ADRs; Phase 1.5+ adds as decisions are made.
