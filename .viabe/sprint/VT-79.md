## What CoderC must produce
The breach detection runbook: a documented playbook for detecting, classifying, containing, and notifying. Plus the automated detectors that fire alerts. Plus the templates Fazal uses to communicate with affected parties.
1. **Detector suite** at `apps/team/src/privacy/breach_detection.py`:
	- **Detector 1: `TenantIsolationViolation` events** — any occurrence in `pipeline_log` triggers immediate critical alert (already wired by VT-8.1; this subtask formalizes the response).
	- **Detector 2: `ContextIsolationViolation` events** — same (VT-8.2).
	- **Detector 3: DSR anomaly** — a tenant's DSR-export rate exceeds 10x typical for their cohort; or a deletion request comes from a phone that hasn't been seen as a customer (potential adversarial probe). Telegram alert.
	- **Detector 4: Cross-tenant phone collision in inbound** — same phone messaging in 2 tenants' channels within 60 seconds (potential session hijack). Telegram alert.
	- **Detector 5: PII pattern in `pipeline_log` payload** — nightly regex sweep on `pipeline_log` looking for unredacted phone numbers, full names, or other PII patterns that should never appear. Critical alert if found.
	- **Detector 6: External access spike** — abnormal rate of `/api/dsr/*` calls from a single IP (potential enumeration attack). Telegram alert + temporary IP throttle.
	- Detectors run as scheduled triggers (VT-3.5) every 30 minutes; PII sweep runs nightly.
2. **Runbook** at `docs/runbooks/breach-response.md`:
	- **Severity classification**:
		- `P0 — confirmed cross-tenant data exposure`: any TenantIsolationViolation or ContextIsolationViolation that the post-mortem confirms reached an actual response. 72-hour DPDPA notification.
		- `P1 — detector fired, exposure likelihood high`: one of the detectors fired on production traffic; investigation required within 4 hours.
		- `P2 — detector fired, exposure likelihood low`: anomalies in dev/test or detector false positives; investigation within 24 hours.
		- `P3 — information event`: PII in logs but redacted in time, or other near-miss; logged + reviewed weekly.
	- **Response procedure** per severity (containment steps, evidence collection, communication templates).
	- **DPDPA notification template** (P0 only): owner-facing + customer-facing notice in English + Hindi.
	- **Post-mortem template**: required for every P0 + P1.
3. **Notification helpers** at `apps/team/src/privacy/breach_notification.py`:
	- `notify_owner(tenant_id, severity, summary)` — sends WhatsApp + email to the owner. Template `breach_notification_owner` (template 14 of 14; Fazal-authored).
	- `notify_customer(customer_id, severity, summary)` — sends WhatsApp to affected customer. Template `breach_notification_customer` (template 15 of 15).
	- `notify_dpdpa_authority(severity, summary)` — currently a manual process (Fazal sends email per Indian Computer Emergency Response Team guidelines); helper writes the email body but Fazal sends. Documented at runbook.
4. **Tests** at `apps/team/tests/privacy/test_breach_detection.py`:
	- Each detector tested with synthetic trigger; correct severity assigned; correct alert path fired.
	- PII regex sweep: synthetic `pipeline_log` row with unredacted phone → detector fires.
	- DSR anomaly: synthetic 100x DSR-rate spike → alert.
	- Cross-tenant phone collision: synthetic 2 inbound messages from same phone within 60s to 2 different tenants → alert.
	- Notification helpers: synthetic owner/customer/authority notification → correct templates dispatched.
	- Runbook completeness check: every severity level has a documented response procedure (manual review by Fazal).
5. **Drill cadence**:
	- Phase 1: tabletop drill once before launch (Fazal + CoderX run through a simulated P0 against the runbook). Documented in `docs/runbooks/breach-response.md` as a sign-off section.
	- Phase 1.5+: quarterly tabletop drills.

## Architectural rules to enforce in this subtask
- Pillar 7: 72-hour DPDPA notification window is non-negotiable. CodeX confirms the runbook documents the timeline.
- All detectors are deterministic. CodeX rejects ML-based anomaly detection (Phase 2 if needed).
- Detector false-positive rate is logged for tuning; PRs adjusting thresholds reference the false-positive history.
- Notification templates are pre-authored. CodeX rejects free-text notification PRs at incident time — templates are vetted in advance.
- Tabletop drill before launch is a Phase 1 deliverable; PR not merged without sign-off section completed.
