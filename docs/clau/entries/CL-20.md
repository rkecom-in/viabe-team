# CL-20 — PII discipline for step records: ONLY customer phone numbers tokenized; everythi

ONLY customer phone numbers get tokenized in step_records (replaced with `cust_tok_<hash>` that resolves through a separate phone_token_resolutions table with stricter RLS). Everything else — transaction amounts, message bodies, campaign decisions, agent reasoning, business names, owner names, locality, business types, dormancy windows, attribution outcomes — goes into step_records in plaintext for full debuggability. Rationale: phone number is the highest-risk leakage vector (one number = identifies a specific human directly), while aggregation of business + amount + message is debuggable context without being a phone book. Clients agreed transparency on agent decisions is necessary for trust + accountability. The phone_token_resolutions table is itself subject to retention policy (90 days), tenant RLS, and is included in the privacy audit log (VT-80) when accessed.
