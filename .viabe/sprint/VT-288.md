# VT-288 — Email + SMS hook channels + cold-start ramp governor

## Why
Inbound-first needs a way to DRIVE customers to message in. Email + SMS are hooks:
a value-message with a click-to-WhatsApp deep link. Plus, a new WABA sending into a
cold list trips Meta's preemptive-enforcement signals (rapid-list-growth /
high-velocity-low-engagement) — so volume must ramp with engagement, not blast.

## What
- DLT-registered SMS templates + email channel: value-hook content → click-to-
  WhatsApp deep link.
- The deep link carries **DURABLE tenant attribution** — server-side resolvable
  (e.g. a short tokenised redirect that maps to tenant), NOT relying on
  user-editable `wa.me` prefilled text.
- Cold-start ramp governor: engagement-led volume ramp (gate daily send volume on
  observed reply/engagement rate) to avoid Meta preemptive enforcement.

## Acceptance criteria
- SMS/email hook send → deep link resolves to the correct tenant server-side
  (attribution survives the user editing the prefilled message).
- Ramp governor caps volume and raises it only as engagement clears thresholds;
  the cap + the decision are observable.
- DLT template registration path documented + canaried.

## Out of scope
- WABA onboarding (VT-286) + the inbound pipeline (VT-287).
- The actual marketing copy (Cowork/Fazal).

## Notes
Plan-first (DLT registration + ramp logic are policy- and enforcement-sensitive).
One coherent PR (CL-424). Plan after VT-286/VT-287 shapes are set.
