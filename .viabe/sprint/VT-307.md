# VT-307 — wire nightly KG-events outbox drain (DBOS.scheduled) + alert route

Fast-follow split out of VT-65 PR-2 (Cowork 20260603T174500Z), sibling of
VT-304/305 (distinct concern — KG drain, not audit-verify / PII-sweep — do NOT
merge them into one handler; shared app_version posture isn't a reason to).

## Why split
VT-65 PR-2 ships the outbox + the **immediate post-commit drain** (canary-proven).
A scheduled sweep is the **reliability backstop** for any straggler (a drain that
failed best-effort, or an event emitted where the immediate drain didn't run).
Registering a `@DBOS.scheduled` handler shifts DBOS app_version (recovery-sensitive)
+ needs its own scheduler canary — hence a tracked follow-up.

## Scope
1. A nightly (off-peak IST) `@DBOS.scheduled` handler sweeping `drain_kg_events`
   across `all_active_tenant_ids` (kg_events with drained_at IS NULL).
2. On repeated drain failure for an event → alert via the VT-202 dispatch path.
3. Scheduler canary: synthetic clock fires the sweep; an undrained outbox row →
   drained to KG; existing handlers' recovery unaffected.

## Dependencies
- VT-65 PR-2 Done (outbox + drain_kg_events shipped).
