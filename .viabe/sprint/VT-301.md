# VT-301 — WhatsApp send-gate (gate ALL business-initiated sends on opt-in)

## Why
Fazal ruled 2026-06-02 (CL-429 Point 1, "Gate all sends"): every business-initiated
WhatsApp template send must require a recorded opt-in. Closes the `TODO(VT-85)` carve-out
in `send_whatsapp_template.py` that left owner-entered customers (VT-55/56/63) sendable
without opt-in. `owner_inputs` (CL-425) is a basis to PROCESS, NOT a WhatsApp opt-in;
owner-entered customers become sendable only after they opt in via the inbound/hook flow
(VT-287 `wa_inbound_optin`).

## What
- `send_whatsapp_template`: after phone resolution, gate on
  `privacy.consent.has_consent_for_phone(tenant_id, phone_e164)`. Fail-CLOSED — no opt-in
  record → refuse `recipient_not_opted_in` (status `unauthorized`), no send.
- IN ADDITION to the existing `opt_out_status` check (CL-421): opt-out still refuses
  `recipient_opted_out` and runs FIRST.
- Applies to ALL customers including owner-entered.

## Acceptance criteria
- Opted-in customer → send allowed (real-PG canary).
- No consent record → send REFUSED fail-closed (`recipient_not_opted_in`), send_fn never called.
- Opted-out → refused (`recipient_opted_out`).
- Cross-tenant: consent under tenant A does NOT authorize a send under tenant B.
- CL-390: refusal logged by code, raw phone never surfaced.

## Out of scope
- Opt-in acquisition surfaces (VT-287 inbound, VT-286 WABA) — already built.
- Backfill/migration of existing customers' consent state.

## Notes
- Canary DR-15 real-PG (no mock cursors): `tests/orchestrator/test_send_gate_optin_realdb.py`
  (4 cases) + mock-pool unit `test_optin_gate_refuses_unconsented`. Decision fn:
  `consent.has_consent_for_phone` → `has_consent` (fail-closed, RLS tenant-scoped).
- NOT launch-blocking. One PR.
