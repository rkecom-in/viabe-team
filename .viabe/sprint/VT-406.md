## Contract (Fazal 2026-06-23 — the load-bearing onboarding fix). DESIGN-FIRST.

**The problem:** discovery anchored "Sundaram Book Store" (owner-typed) to "Sundaram Multi Pap Limited"
(found). Same store here — but unconfirmed. If the anchor entity is wrong, EVERY downstream source
(GST/MCA/IndiaMART/website) is garbage. **Fix: confirm the entity at signup, before discovery runs.**

**Design:** during signup, after business name + city, run a fast candidate lookup (GBP + registries)
and present the **nearest-matching legal + trade names** with disambiguating detail; the owner **selects
the correct one (or "not listed")**. The selected entity becomes the discovery anchor.

## Cowork design flags (raised once; bake into the design)
1. **Graceful "not listed / use my name" path is MANDATORY.** The core Tier-2/3 micro-SMB (a tiny
   bookstore) is NOT in MCA (companies/LLPs only) or GST (below threshold) and may not be on GBP. Entity-
   match must NEVER hard-gate signup — a no-match owner proceeds with their typed name. Don't lose the
   core persona at the door.
2. **False-match risk is real.** Common Indian names (Sundaram, Sharma, Krishna) → many candidates.
   Show address + category + registration so the owner picks the RIGHT one; "none of these" must be easy.
   A wrong anchor is worse than no anchor.
3. **PII boundary (CL-390/425).** Registries carry director/proprietor PII — only business-level data
   enters the profile/VTR view; no director PII; DPDP-aware.
4. **Latency.** Candidate lookup must be fast, or async-with-a-confirm-step — must not stall signup.

## Scope (after design ruling)
1. Signup candidate-match step (lookup + select/verify + not-listed).
2. Persist the confirmed entity (legal name + trade name + registry IDs) as the discovery anchor.
3. Discovery (VT-407) keys off the confirmed entity, not the raw typed name.

## Gate
Design-first → Cowork review (signup-flow + entity-resolution + the no-match path) before build. Plan-first.
