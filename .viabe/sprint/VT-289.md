# VT-289 — OAuth callback state-CSRF hardening (cross-connector)

## Why
Every OAuth-install callback in the codebase currently trusts the URL `state`
param AS the tenant identity (google_sheet `oauth_callback.py`; shopify VT-283
`shopify_oauth.py`). The HMAC proves the redirect came through the provider, but
NOT that *we* initiated the install for that tenant. An attacker constructs their
own authorize URL with `state=<victim_tenant>` + their own shop/WABA, approves it,
and the callback binds THEIR token under the victim's tenant row — an OAuth
account-linking CSRF / tenant-integration hijack on the owner trust boundary.
Flagged HIGH by automated review on VT-283 (#227); Cowork ruling 2026-06-02 accepts
Phase-1 parity now but rosters this hardening as the **hard gate** before any real
owner onboards on an OAuth-install path.

## What
A single-use, server-side `state` nonce bound to the initiating owner session:
- `/setup`-side: authenticate the caller (team-web owner session / internal
  secret), mint `state = secrets.token_urlsafe(32)`, persist a record
  `{state → (tenant_id, owner, connector, shop/waba, expires_at, used=false)}`
  (new table `oauth_install_state`, FORCE RLS, allocator migration), redirect with
  the opaque nonce.
- callback-side: look the nonce up, verify unused + unexpired + connector matches,
  mark used, and derive `tenant_id` from the STORED record — NEVER from the URL.
  Reject unknown/expired/used/forged state.
- Retrofit all three call sites: google_sheet, shopify (#227), and the new
  WhatsApp Embedded Signup (VT-286).

## Acceptance criteria
- Forged/attacker-chosen `state` (not minted by us) → callback rejected (no token
  written).
- A `state` nonce is single-use: replay of a consumed nonce → rejected.
- `tenant_id` is read from the stored nonce record, not the URL.
- Expired nonce → rejected.
- Cross-tenant isolation on `oauth_install_state` (real-PG, synthetic CL-422).
- All three connectors route through the hardened flow; no callback derives tenant
  from the raw URL `state`.

## Out of scope
- The connector business logic itself (owned by VT-283 shopify / VT-286 WABA /
  VT-207 google_sheet) — this row only hardens the state/identity binding.
