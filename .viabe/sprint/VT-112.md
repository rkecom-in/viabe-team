## What CoderC must produce
A decision document evaluating whether Phase 1 sign-up should require KYC verification, AND if yes, vendor selection + integration. The decision precedes implementation. Three vendors compared: DigiLocker (free, government-backed), Karza (paid, full-service), Signzy (paid, lower cost than Karza).
1. **Decision criteria** (documented at `docs/team/kyc-decision.md`):
	- Fraud risk: how likely are fake businesses to sign up for Viabe Team? Phase 1 baseline is unknown (no production data).
	- Friction cost: KYC adds 2-5 minutes to sign-up flow + ~10-30% drop-off rate per industry data.
	- Regulatory pressure: DPDPA does NOT mandate KYC for B2B SMB SaaS; voluntary.
	- Cost: DigiLocker free, Karza ~₹50/verify, Signzy ~₹30/verify.
	- Trust signal: KYC may reassure tenants their data is in a compliance-aware system.
2. **Decision options**:
	- **Option A (RECOMMEND for Phase 1)**: NO KYC at sign-up. Trust comes from Razorpay's payment KYC at trial-to-paid conversion (the founder commits a card; Razorpay's PCI-compliant card capture acts as a soft KYC). Re-evaluate at 100-tenant mark.
	- **Option B**: DigiLocker integration at sign-up. Free, government-backed, but UX friction high (Aadhaar OTP + DigiLocker permission grant).
	- **Option C**: Karza/Signzy at sign-up. Paid, lower friction (background GST/PAN check from public registries), but adds cost per signup.
	- **Option D**: KYC only at first-paid-conversion (Day 14+). Defer the decision until tenant has shown engagement; reduces sign-up friction.
3. **Phase 1 recommendation**: Option A (no KYC) + Razorpay KYC at conversion. Re-evaluate via Phase 1.5 retrospective.
	- Rationale: Phase 1 is small-scale (target 100 tenants); fraud risk is low at this scale; KYC drop-off would hurt acquisition more than fraud would hurt margin; Razorpay handles payment-side KYC.
	- Decision record: `docs/team/kyc-decision.md` with full rationale + Fazal sign-off.
	- Re-evaluation trigger: 50% past 50 tenants OR any fraudulent signup pattern emerges.
4. **If Option B/C chosen**: implementation scaffold for vendor integration (Phase 2 if Option A).
5. **Documentation**: Phase 1 decision documented; vendor evaluation matrix attached for future reference.
