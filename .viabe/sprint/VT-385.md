## Contract (CL-438.2 — design first, build after ruling)

Generalize the Ask/Act principle beyond customer sends: today the ONLY owner-interrupting
surfaces are send approvals (VT-384's lane), the approval defer/budget (VT-334), and
escalations. Design a per-tenant autonomy policy:

1. **Census** every owner-interruption point in the live paths (what asks, when, why).
2. **Action-class taxonomy:** routine/logical (Act-eligible) vs the NON-BYPASSABLE floor
   (opt-out/DSR, consent, money-bearing, suspected monetary/reputational damage,
   anything CL-433/Pillar-7-class) vs knowledge-gaps (VTR lane, CL-426 — NOT owner asks).
3. **Per-tenant `autonomy_mode` (ask|act)** + per-class overrides; Act-mode entry =
   explicit owner opt-in (consent evidence shape, like team_autonomy_offer); standing
   STOP kill applies globally; the run-control panel surfaces mode + lets the VTR see
   (not change) it.
4. **The "suspected damage" tripwire defined mechanically** — what flips an Act action
   back to Ask (detector signals, complaint state, caps breach, validator degradation).
5. plan-ack with the design → Cowork rules → build.
